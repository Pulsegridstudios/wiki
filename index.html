What is an AGR?

An Advanced Gas-cooled Reactor (AGR) is a type of nuclear reactor developed in the United Kingdom. It uses graphite as a neutron moderator and carbon dioxide gas as the coolant — a key design difference from more common water-cooled reactors.

Coolant Loop

The coolant loop is the circulatory system of the reactor. CO₂ gas is pumped through the graphite core at high pressure, absorbing heat from the fuel pins. It then travels to the steam generators where that heat boils water to drive turbines.

Gameplay Note

Players assigned to the Coolant Station must maintain CO₂ flow rate and pressure within safe bands. Deviating too far in either direction triggers cascading alarm events across other crew stations.

Control Rods

Control rods are inserted into the reactor core to absorb neutrons and reduce the rate of fission. In Looped Operation, six control rod banks can be independently adjusted. Full insertion causes a SCRAM — an emergency shutdown.

Crew Roles

Looped Operation is designed around teamwork. Each player takes a specific station, and no single player can run the reactor alone at full efficiency.

01

Reactor Operator

Manages control rod positioning and monitors core neutron flux. Primary decision-maker for power adjustments and SCRAM calls.

02

Coolant Engineer

Controls CO₂ pump speed, gas pressure, and flow rate. Must respond to temperature changes signalled by the Reactor Operator.

03

Turbine Operator

Manages the steam turbines and grid output. Balances load demand with available thermal power from the reactor.

04

Safety Officer

Monitors alarm panels across all systems. First to identify anomalies and coordinate emergency response protocols.
